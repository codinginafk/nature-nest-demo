What is Wax? A Complete Guide to Nature's Most Versatile Material

🕯️ 8 min read📅 June 2026✍️ Nature Nest Team

Wax is one of the oldest materials humans have worked with — dating back over 5,000 years to ancient Egypt, where beeswax was used for everything from shipbuilding to mummification. Today, wax remains an essential part of our daily lives: in the candles that light our homes, the cosmetics we apply, the food we preserve, and the art we create.

What Exactly is Wax?

At its simplest, wax is a class of organic compounds that are malleable at room temperature and melt at relatively low temperatures (typically between 45°C and 100°C). Waxes are hydrophobic — they repel water — which is why they're so useful for waterproofing, sealing, and protecting surfaces. Chemically, most waxes are esters of long-chain fatty acids and long-chain alcohols, though the exact composition varies dramatically depending on the source.

What makes wax special is its unique combination of properties: it's solid at room temperature but melts into a liquid that can be poured, shaped, and molded. When it cools, it holds its new form. This phase-change ability — solid to liquid and back — is what makes wax perfect for candle-making, sculpting, and countless other crafts.

The Major Types of Wax

1. Beeswax — The Original

Beeswax is produced by honeybees to build their honeycomb structures. It's completely natural, non-toxic, and has been used for millennia. Beeswax candles burn brighter and longer than most other waxes, and they emit a subtle, natural honey-like scent. They also release negative ions when burned, which can help purify the air by binding to airborne particles like dust and pollen. At Nature Nest, we use beeswax in our premium blends for its clean burn and warm, golden color.

2. Soy Wax — The Modern Favorite

Soy wax is made from hydrogenated soybean oil and emerged as a popular candle-making wax in the 1990s. It's renewable, biodegradable, and burns cleaner than paraffin — producing significantly less soot. Soy wax also holds fragrance oils exceptionally well, making it ideal for scented candles. It burns slower than paraffin too, so your candle lasts longer. The creamy white appearance of soy wax gives candles a clean, modern look that's become synonymous with artisanal candle brands.

3. Paraffin Wax — The Controversial Workhorse

Paraffin is a petroleum byproduct and the most widely used candle wax globally — largely because it's cheap and holds scent well. However, it's non-renewable and burning paraffin releases potentially harmful compounds like toluene and benzene (though in quantities most studies consider negligible for occasional use). Many artisan candle makers — including Nature Nest — choose to avoid paraffin entirely in favor of natural alternatives.

4. Coconut Wax — The Luxury Choice

Coconut wax is made from cold-pressed coconut oil and is one of the most sustainable wax options. It burns extremely slowly and cleanly, with excellent scent throw (both hot and cold). Coconut wax has a beautiful creamy texture and is often blended with soy or beeswax for the perfect candle consistency.

5. Palm Wax — The Crystalline Beauty

Palm wax creates stunning crystalline or "feathered" patterns as it cools, making it a favorite for decorative candles. However, sustainability concerns around palm oil production mean it's crucial to source only RSPO-certified (Roundtable on Sustainable Palm Oil) palm wax. At Nature Nest, we use certified sustainable palm wax sparingly and only when the unique crystalline effect is desired.

6. Gel Wax — The Transparent Option

Gel wax isn't technically a wax at all — it's a mineral oil thickened with polymer resin. It's transparent, which makes it perfect for embedding decorative objects (sea shells, dried flowers, glitter) inside the candle. It burns about twice as long as paraffin but requires special wicks and fragrance oils compatible with its chemistry.

Why Wax Choice Matters for Candles

The type of wax determines almost everything about a candle: how long it burns, how well it throws scent, how clean the burn is, and even how the candle looks. A cheap paraffin candle might cost 10 AED but burn unevenly, produce soot, and smell synthetic. A handcrafted soy-and-beeswax candle at 48 AED delivers 45+ hours of clean, fragrant burn — and you can feel good about what you're breathing in.

At Nature Nest, we blend soy wax and beeswax in our signature formula: the soy gives us clean burn and scent retention, while the beeswax adds burn time, natural sweetness, and that warm golden glow. No paraffin. No shortcuts. Just wax the way nature intended.
