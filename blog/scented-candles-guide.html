The Complete Guide to Scented Candles

🕯️ 8 min read📅 June 2026✍️ Nature Nest Team

A scented candle is more than just wax and fragrance — it's an experience. The right scent at the right moment can calm anxiety, sharpen focus, trigger cherished memories, or simply make a room feel like home. There's neuroscience behind this: scent is the only sense directly wired to the brain's limbic system — the seat of emotion and memory — which is why a whiff of lavender can instantly transport you to your grandmother's garden.

But not all scented candles are created equal. The difference between a 20 AED supermarket candle and a 65 AED handcrafted one isn't just branding — it's chemistry, ingredient quality, and craft. Let's break down everything that goes into a truly great scented candle.

How Scented Candles Work: Fragrance Oils vs. Essential Oils

The scent in a candle comes from fragrance oils or essential oils blended into the wax. When the candle burns, heat releases these volatile compounds into the air — this is called the "hot throw." A good candle also has a "cold throw" — the scent you smell when the candle isn't lit, just sitting in the room.

Essential Oils

Essential oils are extracted directly from plants — lavender from lavender flowers, eucalyptus from eucalyptus leaves, citrus oils from fruit peels. They're 100% natural, which is a big selling point. However, essential oils have limitations: they're expensive, the scent can be subtle, and some essential oils don't perform well at the high temperatures of a burning candle (citrus oils, in particular, can burn off quickly).

Fragrance Oils

Fragrance oils are blended specifically for candle-making. They can be synthetic, natural, or (most commonly) a blend of both. High-quality fragrance oils are designed to perform at candle temperatures, throw scent consistently throughout the burn, and offer enormous creative range — from "fresh linen" to "amber and oud" to complex perfumery-like blends. They're also rigorously safety-tested for candle use.

At Nature Nest, we use primarily pure essential oils in our candles, supplemented with phthalate-free, high-quality fragrance oils for consistency and throw. Every scent we create is tested across multiple burn cycles to ensure it smells just as wonderful on hour 40 as it does on hour 1.

The Art of Scent Throw

"Scent throw" is the candle-maker's term for how well a candle distributes fragrance. Getting it right is a balancing act:

  • Too little fragrance oil → Weak scent, disappointing. The candle smells like nothing.
  • Too much fragrance oil → The wax can't bind it all. Oil pools on the surface, creating a fire hazard and uneven burn.
  • Wrong wax type → Some waxes hold fragrance better than others. Soy wax is excellent; paraffin is good but synthetic-feeling; beeswax has its own natural scent that competes with added fragrance.
  • Wrong wick size → The wick controls the flame temperature. Too hot and the fragrance burns off too fast; too cool and it never fully releases.

The sweet spot is typically 6-10% fragrance oil by weight, depending on the wax blend and the specific fragrance. We test every new scent at multiple concentrations before it makes it into our collection.

Choosing the Right Scent for Your Space

For Relaxation & Sleep

Lavender is the gold standard — studies consistently show it lowers heart rate and blood pressure. Chamomile, vanilla, and sandalwood are also excellent for creating calm. Our Calm Collection Set pairs chamomile and vanilla for the perfect evening wind-down.

For Focus & Energy

Citrus scents (lemon, bergamot, grapefruit) are uplifting and energizing. Peppermint and rosemary improve alertness and cognitive performance. Eucalyptus clears the mind and opens the airways — great for a home office.

For Romance & Ambiance

Rose, jasmine, ylang-ylang, and amber create warmth and intimacy. Woody notes like cedar and sandalwood add depth. These are scents that make a room feel inhabited and inviting.

For Freshness & Cleanliness

Linen, cotton, sea salt, and light citrus (bergamot, lemongrass) evoke cleanliness and openness. Perfect for kitchens, bathrooms, and entryways.

Synthetic vs. Natural: What's Actually Better?

The honest answer: it depends. Pure essential oils are beautiful and natural, but a well-crafted fragrance oil can create scent experiences that essential oils alone can't achieve — "sea salt and driftwood," "leather and tobacco," "fresh baked bread" — none of these exist as essential oils. What matters most is transparency: we tell you exactly what's in our candles and why. No mystery "fragrance" listed — specific notes, specific sources.

The only hard rule: avoid phthalates. Some synthetic fragrance oils use phthalates as fixatives, and these compounds are known endocrine disruptors. Every fragrance component we use is phthalate-free, period.
